01 · Research question

Precision changes success sets—but “control” is still a hypothesis.

FP16 and BNB4 solve overlapping but non-identical subsets. The new question is whether this is a precision-specific trajectory effect or merely another form of decoding noise.

A valid control-variable thesis requires a negative temperature-equivalence result, independent replication, and utility-aware routing that accounts for harmful FP switches.

  1. 1How does precision change reasoning trajectories?
  2. 2Is the effect distinct from temperature sampling?
  3. 3Can we predict beneficial versus harmful switches?
  4. 4Do early signals justify token-time intervention?

05 · What we learned

The evaluator is part of the experiment.

01

Aggregate accuracy hides churn.

FP16 and BNB4 can have identical accuracy while succeeding on different examples. Adaptive precision should optimize counterfactual utility, not simply imitate FP.

02

Interventions can be harmful.

FP correct/Q wrong cases add accuracy, but FP wrong/Q correct cases remove it. Controller evaluation now counts both.

03

Formatting can masquerade as reasoning.

Token caps and fallback-to-last-number extraction contaminated the first run. The revised prompt, parser, and stop conditions target this directly.

04

Small AUCs need humility.

The first predictor had only five positive test failures. Held-out evaluation and bootstrap intervals are now mandatory.

06 · Novelty assessment

The broad idea is not novel. A narrower contribution may be.

As of June 2026, multiple papers already perform dynamic mixed-precision LLM inference or selective high-precision recovery. The thesis must be positioned around correctness-risk and counterfactual intervention utility, not around “dynamic precision” alone.

Closest workOverlapRemaining distinction
FlexQuant (2025)Per-token/layer switching using entropy, KL, and top-logit toleranceOptimizes token fidelity and speed, not paired reasoning correctness utility or harmful fallback
DP-LLM (2025)Runtime layer-wise bit selection from lightweight input-error estimatesTargets precision/latency adaptation rather than calibrated critical-error prediction
Quantization Meets Reasoning (2025)Localizes early reasoning failures and restores local token marginsUses curated fine-tuning rather than an online intervention/abstention policy
Extreme Low-Bit Inference (2026)Detects generation pathologies and selectively falls back to FP16Focuses on 2-bit loops and planning; our proposed target is general correctness risk at modest 4-bit precision
ReQAT (2026)Finds reasoning failures at critical low-entropy symbolic commitmentsTraining-based W4A4KV4 recovery; our evidence is quant-only risk prediction for W4A16
Quantization Inflates Reasoning (2026)Shows quantization increases reasoning-token usage and serving costOur 1.081× token inflation is a small-model replication, not a novel claim
Defensible thesis direction: predict the signed counterfactual value of a precision intervention—beneficial, neutral, or harmful—from quantized-only signals, then optimize risk–coverage–cost with optional abstention. The signed controller is now implemented, but entropy still wins at the 10% end-to-end budget; this is a current negative result, not a solved contribution.

This is a scoped literature assessment, not proof of priority. Read the from-scratch project guide and full statistical and novelty audit.
